Aadi's Jukebox

A collection of my favorite albums! Lots of hip hop, trap metal, and underground favorites. Hovering over any of the icons will open a little card detailing why I like that album so much as well as a link to listen to it. These first three are my most recommended listens.

"Psychonauts" by Brahman

This album is so underground that it was deleted in 2018 and only resurfaced in 2025 because some guy happened to have the files downloaded on his computer and uploaded it to a youtube playlist. Despite it's obscurity, it is an insanely good album! Maximalistic psychedelic boom bap production rapped over by a guy that's able to match the vibe perfectly. Overall a very consistently good album that starts off very strong and is able to remain solid throughout the entire tracklist.

Favorite Songs: Psychonauts, Shroomsville, Vertigo, 420 degrees, Cruisin'


"And They Mine for Our Bodies" by Gao The Arsonist

Self-written and self-produced. Every song transitions into the next. Each song is based on a different part of an overarching story the artist writes about in a seperate manga. It is UNBELIEVABLE how much thought is put into every single part of the music. It also just sounds like nothing else I've heard before, it really feels like its own genre. It's one of those albums that has its own mood that it imparts onto you, making it really feel like a cohesive project.

Favorite Songs: Insomania, Right Between the Eyes, Unorthodox


"There's Evil in This Club" by Christ Dillinger

Really fun album that's kind of a fusion between disco and rap, with random Shadow Wizard Money gang tags scattered throughout. Not sure what else to say, it's just a very fun listen. The production is great, the rapping is great, all of the songs are in the same genre so it really feels like it's just one focused album, everything meshes together. The fact that it's only six songs long means that there's no space for filler tracks, each song has a good reason for being there.

Favorite Songs: The album is only seven songs just listen to the whole thing
