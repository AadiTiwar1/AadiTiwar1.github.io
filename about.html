Longer About Me


The following is my CommonApp essay. I wrote it in 2023 in the midst of high school college applications, so it might seem overdramatic since it was written to appeal to admissions officers, but I feel that to this day it continues to do a pretty good job at describing my lifelong interests in Quantum Physics & Computing and why I'm pursuing what I am.

PROMPT: Some students have a background, identity, interest, or talent that is so meaningful they believe their application would be incomplete without it. If this sounds like you, then please share your story.

While many people treasure important moments or days in their lives, I treasure my most creative thought, one I had at 7 years old: how cool would it be to live in a world with teleporters? I started daydreaming about how much better the world would be with teleporters: no more late slips at school, no more pollution, meeting friends whenever I wanted. Wondering why one didn’t exist in real life, I was led down a rabbit hole of Wikipedia pages with words I hardly understood until I found the page for Quantum Physics.

I remember reading the Quantum Physics page and immediately being captured - things like quantum entanglement and tunneling seemed straight out of a fantasy movie, even though I couldn't understand any of it. I put the article down hoping to one day understand this magical field I’d stumbled upon in greater depth.

A few years later in 9th grade, the computing aspect to my Quantum Physics obsession began. Growing up in the competitive Silicon Valley computer science environment, I was always told that becoming a software engineer would solve any problem I’d ever have in life. Admittedly, the problem-solving aspect of computer science was unique and it had real world applications. Yet, computer science didn’t feel like something I could spend the rest of my life pursuing. It felt like it was something that was forced on to me rather than something I chose out on my own. I became disinterested, desperate for something that was unique and enjoyable.

A few months later, I came across a news article: “Quantum Computing: The Technology of the Future.” It described taking the wondrous properties of quantum physics I once wanted to master as a child and fusing it with the problem-solving and applicability of computer science to create something that I could call mine, something that’d allow me to pursue my childhood dreams. I realized that Quantum Computing was my calling. Lit with the fire of a newfound passion, I was set on immersing myself as deeply into the world of quantum computing as a high schooler possibly could.

Immediately, no other topic I’d ever learned fascinated me as much as this one did. It felt like every new fact about superposition or matrix representations of a quantum logic gate got me one step closer to harnessing a superpower, an unparalleled feeling.

I’d start sleeping later and later learning more and more as I tried to learn to cast the spells of this magic called quantum physics. And eventually, I was starting to get somewhere. The magical properties which I was so spellbound by as a child made sense to me, and it now seemed like the field was my oyster. I really felt like I’d started making it when a UCLA professor saw enough in me to take me on as a first-author for an official research paper last summer [this essay was written in Fall 2023], all because I once had a question about teleporters.

I continue to pursue Quantum Physics & Computing as it's not just a 7 year old's daydreams anymore - it's the dreams of me yesterday, today, and tomorrow. There is so much room for innovation and creativity in this nascent field, and I strive to one day be remembered as a pioneer in the field.
